\abstract{The use of simplified models of dark matter is becoming increasingly prevalent in collider searches, and while early Run II results are beginning to appear, we look to see what further information can be extracted from Run I results. We consider three `standard' simplified models that couple quarks to fermionic singlet dark matter: an $s$-channel vector mediator with vector or axial-vector couplings, and a $t$-channel scalar mediator. Upper limits on the couplings are calculated, and compared across three alternate channels, namely, mono-jet, mono-$Z$ (leptonic) and mono-$W/Z$ (hadronic). The strongest limits are observed in the mono-jet channel, however the computational simplicity, and absence of significant $t$-channel model width effects, in the mono-boson channels make these a straightforward and fast alternative.}
